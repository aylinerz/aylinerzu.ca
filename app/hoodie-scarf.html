Context

In this colaboration between OCADU and Yonge Street Mission. We tasked to design a soft good product that can be produced locally by group of immigrant women.

The Hoodie-Scarf was merging of their middle eastern culture and Canadian cullture. It is designed to keep you warm while keping you stylish, and the form of the scarf resembles the head scarves middle easter women wear.

User

It is mainly designed for new comers who may want some extra layers to stay warm on Canada's cold winters! It is a very simple design that prevents you from carrying a hat and a scarf seperately. The bright red colour is symbol to celebrate and appreciate Canada, and the the long legth allows for lots of bundling up. The inspiration for the Hoodie-Scarf came from the common dislike towards chilly Canadian weather.

Process

While designing Hoodie-Scarf staying within budget and skill sets of the women from CWC was esential. It also had to be easy and fast to produce since they were mainly gonna sew at home, during their family time. I wanted the product I created to be relatable to their life styles and something they would enjoy sewing.

Prototype

Initially Hoodie scarf was planned to be upcycled from old scarves. However, when i started looking for material I realized i would be hard to find matching patterns and colours. Therefore I quickly switched to using polar in the inside and wool on the outside and sewing it out of scratch.

Exhibition

It was a privilege to display my work at the OCAD U X YSM Regent Park Exhibition and the Toronto Offsite Design Festival. I was also awarded the 'Love Tag' by DesignLines Magazine.
