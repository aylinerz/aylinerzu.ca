The Velo-Jacket

Goal

In this project we were requested to create a user-centric design, using button or motion sensor controlled Arduino and a LEDs. The intent was to design something that would make user’s life easier.

Personal Goal:

After researching how technology is being utilized to help and ease our daily lives, I wanted my design to be;

  • Used in everyday life
  • Appropriate to Canadian life style
  • Solve an existing problem (with an inexpensive solution)
  • Easy to use (as fool proof as possible)

Research

In our contemporary daily life bikes are being used not only for recreational purposes, but also more and more as a mean of transportation. Despite all safety measures such as, bike roads, helmets and attachable lights, the number of serious injuries is still on the rise. After interviewing some cyclists and drivers I discovered the majority is not comfortable sharing the road, especially during dark hours when hand signals are less visible.

Result

The Velo Jacket is designed to add another layer of protection by making the cyclists more noticeable to other vehicles in the traffic. The jacket is mainly designed for downtown Toronto cyclists who need to be extra cautious while sharing the road with other vehicles in the dark. It is designed so as to make your turn signals more visible at night.

The right and left LED arrows at the back are powered by a washable Arduino board (LilyPad) and are activated by two buttons placed on the jacket sleeves. The red and green coloured LED pins are inspired by aircraft navigation lights (red arrow light indicating the left and green arrow light indicating the right).

Skills

During the test period, I examined different Arduino boards and their specific qualities. I also tried out different switches and sensors such as the tilt break switch and simple push buttons so as to find the most suitable component combination. In order to have the signaling to function as required, I learned the usege of Arduino IDE, LilyPads and their coding.
