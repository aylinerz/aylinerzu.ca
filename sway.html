Goal

Kids naturally love beanbag chairs, they are fun to play around with and they are a safer option compared to rigid and hard furniture. Along with being fun to use for everyone, Sway turns already existing beanbag chairs in to rocking chairs. It holds the beanbag chair in place providing extra support to the body.

Context

TDSB Special Needs School* provides a wide variety of individualized programs for the educational needs of students that have developmental and/or physical disabilities.

User

Children with special needs have specific support and therapy needs that ordinary chairs are unable to provide. Their positioning needs are unique to the way each of their bodies develop. Beanbag chairs retain their shape and hug individual's body as needed to provide deep pressure input for a calming effect.

Definition

Is it weird to occasionally miss being held like a child as a functioning adult?

Sway, combined with a beanbag, is an amazing tool that relieves stress by creating a sense of security not just for special needs kids but for everyone.

Health Benefits

Gentle rocking motion releases endorphins in the brain which reduces stress, that enhances emotional, social and cognitive development.

Increases blood flow around the body, sending more oxygen to the joints, helping build strength and flexibility.

According to a study conducted, patients, with dementia, that used a rocking chair showed increase in emotional and psychological wellbeing as well as an improvement in their balance.

Process

Most constraining part of this project was trying to stay within budget. It was interesting to see how the limited budget changed the look of Sway so drastically. During ideation, we initially planned on building it with vacuum formed plastic, but due to budget constraints, we switched to steam bending wood. Ultimately, the most durable way to build sway, while being cost effective is to build it with ribs.
